This came from one this morning and I appreciated it a bit:

The zen of self-improvement

Many who read this blog and others like it are dedicated to self-improvement. Dedication to self-improvement is an interesting phenomena; it can be a subtle form of self-criticism disguised by noble intentions, of never being good enough yet.

Zen teachers have long said that the right intention for meditation is not meditation in order to gain peace and joy. As long as we believe our true nature is otherwise, we will never attain it. The paradox of zen style "self-improvement" is that we can only gain deeper experiences of peace and joy to the extent that we realize the peace and joy we already are.

We don't meditate to be enlightened; we meditate because we're enlightened. The aim of practice - whatever form that takes - is to become more of who we already are, not to become someone or something else.
